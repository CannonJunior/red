SECTION C - DESCRIPTION/SPECIFICATIONS/WORK STATEMENT
================================================================================

1.0 BACKGROUND

The Department of Defense requires comprehensive Information Technology support
services to maintain and enhance the Joint All-Domain Command and Control system.

2.0 SCOPE OF WORK

This contract encompasses all labor, materials, equipment, and services necessary
to provide complete IT support for the JADC2 infrastructure.

3.0 TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

3.1 SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE AND INFRASTRUCTURE

3.1.1 The contractor shall design and implement a cloud-based architecture that
supports seamless integration across all military domains including air, land,
sea, space, and cyber operations.

3.1.2 The system must achieve 99.9% uptime during operational hours with mean
time to recovery (MTTR) not exceeding 4 hours for critical system failures.

3.1.3 All infrastructure components shall be hosted in FedRAMP High authorized
cloud environments approved by the Department of Defense.

3.2 SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

3.2.1 The contractor shall implement and maintain security controls in accordance
with NIST SP 800-53 Revision 5 at the High baseline level.

3.2.2 All data at rest must be encrypted using FIPS 140-2 validated cryptographic
modules with AES-256 encryption algorithms.

3.2.3 Data in transit shall be protected using TLS 1.3 or higher protocols with
Perfect Forward Secrecy (PFS) enabled.

3.2.4 The system will implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all user
access using CAC/PIV cards and biometric verification methods.

3.2.5 Security vulnerability scans must be conducted monthly with critical
vulnerabilities remediated within 30 days and high vulnerabilities within 90 days.

3.3 INTEROPERABILITY AND INTEGRATION

3.3.1 The contractor shall ensure the system integrates with existing DoD
enterprise systems including: Defense Information Systems Network (DISN), Global
Command and Control System (GCCS), and Distributed Common Ground System (DCGS).

3.3.2 All application programming interfaces (APIs) must conform to DoD Enterprise
DevSecOps Reference Design specifications.

3.3.3 The system should support real-time data sharing across coalition partner
networks when authorized by appropriate security authorities.

3.4 PERFORMANCE AND SCALABILITY

3.4.1 The system shall support concurrent access by at least 10,000 authenticated
users without performance degradation.

3.4.2 Database query response times must not exceed 100 milliseconds for 95% of
transactions under normal load conditions.

3.4.3 The architecture shall be designed to scale horizontally to accommodate
50% growth in user base and data volume within 24 hours.

3.5 DATA MANAGEMENT

3.5.1 The contractor shall implement automated backup procedures with recovery
point objective (RPO) of 15 minutes and recovery time objective (RTO) of 1 hour
for mission-critical data.

3.5.2 Data retention policies must comply with DoD 5015.2 STD records management
requirements.

3.5.3 The system should provide audit logging capabilities that capture all user
actions with tamper-evident storage for minimum 7 years.

4.0 SUPPORT AND MAINTENANCE

4.1 TECHNICAL SUPPORT

4.1.1 The contractor shall provide 24x7x365 technical support with tier 1 response
within 15 minutes for critical incidents.

4.1.2 A dedicated program manager must be available during normal business hours
(0800-1700 EST) Monday through Friday excluding federal holidays.

4.1.3 The contractor should maintain a knowledge base of common issues and
resolutions accessible to both support staff and end users.

4.2 MAINTENANCE AND UPDATES

4.2.1 Security patches shall be evaluated within 48 hours of vendor release and
deployed to production within 14 days following successful testing.

4.2.2 System maintenance windows may be scheduled monthly during off-peak hours
(0200-0600 EST) with 72-hour advance notice to government representatives.

4.3 TRAINING AND DOCUMENTATION

4.3.1 The contractor shall develop and maintain comprehensive system documentation
including architecture diagrams, standard operating procedures, and troubleshooting
guides.

4.3.2 Training courses must be provided for three user levels: end users, system
administrators, and security personnel.

4.3.3 Annual refresher training should be conducted for all system administrators
to address new features and updated procedures.

5.0 DELIVERABLES

5.1 The contractor shall provide the following deliverables:

5.1.1 System Design Document within 30 days after contract award.
5.1.2 Security Assessment Report quarterly.
5.1.3 Monthly status reports summarizing system performance, incidents, and
planned activities.
5.1.4 Annual disaster recovery test results demonstrating successful failover
and restoration procedures.

SECTION L - INSTRUCTIONS, CONDITIONS, AND NOTICES TO OFFERORS
================================================================================

1.0 PROPOSAL SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

1.1 GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS

1.1.1 Proposals shall be submitted electronically through the System for Award
Management (SAM.gov) portal by the deadline specified in Section A.

1.1.2 Late proposals will not be considered unless waived by the Contracting
Officer due to circumstances beyond the offeror's control.

1.1.3 Proposals must be submitted in PDF format with individual file sizes not
exceeding 50 megabytes.

1.2 PROPOSAL ORGANIZATION

1.2.1 The technical proposal shall be organized in the following order: executive
summary, technical approach, management approach, past performance, and staffing
plan.

1.2.2 The cost proposal must be submitted separately from the technical proposal
and include detailed cost breakdowns by CLIN, labor category, and other direct
costs.

1.2.3 Page limits for the technical proposal are 50 pages including graphics but
excluding cover page, table of contents, and resumes.

2.0 TECHNICAL PROPOSAL REQUIREMENTS

2.1 TECHNICAL APPROACH

2.1.1 Offerors shall describe their proposed solution architecture addressing all
requirements specified in Section C including cloud infrastructure, security
implementation, and integration approach.

2.1.2 The technical approach must demonstrate understanding of JADC2 operational
concepts and multi-domain integration challenges.

2.1.3 Risk mitigation strategies should be provided for identified technical
challenges and potential obstacles.

2.2 MANAGEMENT APPROACH

2.2.1 Offerors shall provide an organizational chart showing proposed project
structure and reporting relationships.

2.2.2 Quality assurance procedures must be described including testing
methodologies, defect tracking, and continuous improvement processes.

2.3 STAFFING PLAN

2.3.1 Key personnel resumes shall include current security clearance levels and
relevant certifications such as CISSP, CISM, or DoD 8570.01-M requirements.

2.3.2 Labor mix should demonstrate appropriate balance of senior, mid-level, and
junior staff to accomplish required tasks cost-effectively.

3.0 PAST PERFORMANCE

3.1.1 Offerors shall provide at least three references for projects of similar
scope, complexity, and technical requirements completed within the past 3 years.

3.1.2 Each reference must include contract number, customer point of contact,
contract value, period of performance, and brief description of work performed.

4.0 QUESTIONS AND AMENDMENTS

4.1.1 Questions regarding this solicitation must be submitted in writing through
SAM.gov no later than February 1, 2025.

4.1.2 Offerors are responsible for monitoring SAM.gov for amendments to this
solicitation.

SECTION M - EVALUATION FACTORS FOR AWARD
================================================================================

1.0 EVALUATION METHODOLOGY

1.1 The Government will evaluate proposals using a best-value tradeoff process
considering both technical and cost factors.

1.2 Technical factors are significantly more important than cost when determining
best value for the Government.

2.0 EVALUATION FACTORS

2.1 FACTOR 1: TECHNICAL APPROACH (Weight: 40%)

2.1.1 The Government shall evaluate the proposed solution architecture for
feasibility, innovation, and compliance with stated requirements.

2.1.2 Integration approach with existing DoD systems will be assessed for
technical soundness and risk mitigation.

2.1.3 Proposed security implementation must demonstrate deep understanding of
FedRAMP High and NIST 800-53 requirements.

2.2 FACTOR 2: MANAGEMENT APPROACH (Weight: 25%)

2.2.1 The Government shall evaluate the project management plan for adequacy of
oversight, communication procedures, and quality assurance processes.

2.2.2 Proposed organizational structure should demonstrate clear lines of
authority and efficient decision-making processes.

2.3 FACTOR 3: PAST PERFORMANCE (Weight: 20%)

2.3.1 Past performance shall be evaluated based on quality of work, schedule
adherence, cost control, and customer satisfaction.

2.3.2 Recent and relevant contracts will be weighted more heavily than older or
less relevant experience.

2.3.3 Offerors with no past performance history may be evaluated neutrally
neither favorably nor unfavorably.

2.4 FACTOR 4: KEY PERSONNEL QUALIFICATIONS (Weight: 15%)

2.4.1 The Government shall assess the qualifications, experience, and security
clearances of proposed key personnel.

2.4.2 Relevant certifications and specialized training in JADC2 or similar
multi-domain systems will be considered favorably.

3.0 COST EVALUATION

3.1.1 The Government will evaluate proposed costs for realism, reasonableness,
and completeness.

3.1.2 Unrealistically low prices may result in proposal rejection if determined
to reflect insufficient understanding of requirements.

4.0 AWARD DECISION

4.1.1 Award shall be made to the offeror whose proposal represents the best value
to the Government considering technical merit and cost.

4.1.2 The Government may conduct discussions with offerors in the competitive
range or make award without discussions.
